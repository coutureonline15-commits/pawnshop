Terms of Pawn: A pawn loan is a short-term financial instrument. For long term-financial needs, we recommend exploring alternative sources of credit.

Should an item be sold or otherwise transferred to a third party before the loan is paid in full (e.g., a "Rent to Own" agreement), The Pawner/Seller acknowledges that they may be subject to criminal prosecution or civil liability.

Loss of Pawn Ticket: The Pawner/Seller must notify Jay's Pawn Shop immediately if the pawn ticket is lost or stolen. The property rights in the articles is exclusively evidenced by the pawn ticket. Any individual presenting the ticket my redeem articles upon full payment of the pawn loan.

Forfeiture and Sale: If the articles pledged are not redeemed within 30 from the date of the pawn, they will be forfeited, without notice, and sold. The loan will be satisfied by the all proceeds of this sale, which shall belong entirely to the pawnshop. The Due Date may be extended by paying the required pawn fee. Verbal agreements regarding extensions are not binding..

Limitation of Liability: The holder of the pawn ticket agrees and acknowledges that the pawnshop shall not be liable for the loss of or damage to the pawned items caused by fire, breakage, or burglary

Accepted Forms of Payment: Accepted Forms of Payment for loans are cash, bank draft, money order, or electronic transfer (credit/debit card)
